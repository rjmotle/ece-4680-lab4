The theme of this work is the idea of the Aeon (Greek, Aion). My investigation seeks, with the help of Christian, Gnostic, and alchemical symbols of the self, to throw light on the change of psychic situation within the "Christian aeon."
[In the Swiss edition, the foreword begins slightly differently with a poetic quote from Hölderlin about "liberty" (Freiheit) rising anew, but the English translation uses a more direct version.]
Part I, entitled The Archetypes and the Collective Unconscious, is composed of shorter essays; Part II, Aion, is a long monograph on the archetype of the self.

1 The ego is the subject of all successful attempts at adaptation so far as these are achieved by the will. The ego is consequently the essential basis of the will.
2 The ego is a relatively late product of ontogenetic development. It is the product of numerous experiences and adaptations, and it is distinguished by a continuity of consciousness that enables it to appear as a unity. This continuity is interrupted only in pathological states, such as somnambulism, double personality, schizophrenia, etc.
3 The ego is the centre of consciousness and the carrier of the empirical personality. It is the subject of cognition and volition, and as such it is the indispensable condition of all conscious psychic processes.
4 The ego is not identical with the totality of the psyche, but only with a limited part of it. The unconscious is everything that is not included in the ego-complex.
5 The ego arises from the collision between somatic and psychic factors on the one hand and environmental influences on the other. It is a product of differentiation within the original unconscious wholeness.
